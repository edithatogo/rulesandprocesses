# Source Discovery Protocol

Source discovery is the first step for every process-profile pathway, before
interpretation, mapping, or execution design.

1. Identify potentially relevant current primary or official policy documents
   for the jurisdiction, service context, process purpose, and event type.
2. Search official issuer and legislation/regulator domains, including current
   guidance, amendments, replacements, local overlays, and implementation
   documents.
3. Verify each candidate against the authoritative document, not a search
   result, summary, or uncited commentary.
4. Record issuer, authority class, jurisdiction, scope, effective date,
   supersession, retrieval time, rights, URL, and digest in the source ledger.
5. Only then create or update a source assertion. Unverified candidates remain
   discovery leads and cannot support a disposition or obligation.

The discovery step is a human-controlled research task. Deterministic tooling
may validate completeness, provenance, dates, and source-state transitions, but
does not decide legal, clinical, organisational, or policy meaning.
